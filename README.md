## Judging Criteria

the Hackathon will have specific judging criteria which will guide judges and participants. 

**Technological Implementation**: Judges assess how well you've utilized the required tools and technologies. They'll also consider the complexity and technical sophistication of your project. Don't be afraid to push boundaries and showcase your technical expertise.

**Ease of use**: A great project isn't just technically impressive, it's also user-friendly. Judges will look for an intuitive design and a smooth user experience. Make sure your project is easy to navigate and understand.

Tip: Put yourself in the user's shoes. If you can't explain how to use your project in a sentence or two, it might be too complicated.

Demonstration: Your demo video is your chance to shine. It should clearly and concisely explain your project, its features, and its benefits. A well-crafted demo can significantly boost your chances of winning.

Tip: Check out this blog post for tips on making a winning demo video from past hackathon winners!

Potential impact: Does your project solve a genuine problem? Does it have the potential to make a positive impact? If so, make sure to highlight this in your submission.

Tip: Research your problem area thoroughly. Understanding the landscape helps you articulate your project's potential impact.

Quality of the Idea: Originality and innovation are highly valued in hackathons. Don't be afraid to think outside the box and come up with unique solutions.

‍Design: A clean, functional, and professional design can go a long way. Pay attention to the aesthetics of your project and make sure it is presented well.

Tip: You can make a great project design even if you’re not a designer, check out this blog post to learn more about how to use AI to elevate your submission.

Additional tips for success
Check the rules page: Seriously, read the rules page carefully. It will outline all the eligibility and submission requirements.

Theme inspiration: If the hackathon has a theme, use it as inspiration for your project. This will demonstrate your understanding of the hackathon's goals and increase the relevance of your submission.

Practice Your Pitch: If there's a presentation component, practice your pitch beforehand. A confident and well-rehearsed presentation can make a strong impression on the judges.
