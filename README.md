## Judging Criteria

the Hackathon will have specific judging criteria which will guide judges and participants. 

**Technological Implementation**: Judges assess how well you've utilized the required tools and technologies. They'll also consider the complexity and technical sophistication of your project. Don't be afraid to push boundaries and showcase your technical expertise.

**Ease of use**: A great project isn't just technically impressive, it's also user-friendly. Judges will look for an intuitive design and a smooth user experience. Make sure your project is easy to navigate and understand.

**Tip**: Put yourself in the user's shoes. If you can't explain how to use your project in a sentence or two, it might be too complicated.

**Demonstration**: the presentation a participants chance to shine. participants should clearly and concisely explain their project, its features, and its benefits. A well-crafted demo can significantly boost chances of winning.

**Potential impact**: Does the project solve a genuine problem? Does it have the potential to make a positive impact?

**Quality of the Idea**: Originality and innovation are highly valued in hackathons. Don't be afraid to think outside the box and come up with unique solutions.

**‍Design**: A clean, functional, and professional design can go a long way. Pay attention to the aesthetics of projects and make sure it is presented well.
